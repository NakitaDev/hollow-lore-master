# Higher Beings

Source: https://hollowknight.fandom.com/wiki/Higher_Beings

Higher beings are god-like creatures in the world of Hallownest and Pharloom that exist above all others. They have certain abilities and powers that regular bugs do not; although, these abilities are generally different for each higher being. In addition to this, some higher beings were deified; worshipped as gods by other bugs.

"Pale Being" is a term which refers to two higher beings, the Pale King and the White Lady . The Kingsoul , a holy Charm containing limitless Soul within, is a symbol of their union. The rare metal, Pale Ore , shares some unknown connection with these pale beings as well.

The Pale King

The Pale King is a Wyrm who transformed from his Wyrm form into his current form in order to match the bugs of Hallownest; the corpse of this Wyrm can still be found in Kingdom's Edge . He is described as being a "light". He is able to expand the minds of bugs, giving them sapience. Like all Wyrms, he also has the ability of foresight. The Pale King was the monarch of Hallownest but disappeared sometime after the Infection returned. The Pale King’s corpse can be found in the White Palace . The Godseeker notes that he was completely erased but that his power still lingers.

The White Lady

The White Lady is a Root who was the Queen of Hallownest. She has an insatiable desire to spread her seeds and to propagate herself, which resulted in the process of Vessels being born. It is unknown if she has any other powers, but she does have the ability to create flowers. The White Lady bound herself in the Queen's Gardens to stop herself from having more offspring. The White Lady seems to be evading attunement by the Godseeker due to her diminishing her power.

The Radiance

The Radiance is a higher being who created the Moth Tribe . She is described as being a light brighter than Essence , which is the substance that dreams are made of. After she was almost entirely forgotten, she started exerting her power through the dreams of bugs, leading to the Infection . In response to this, the Pale King sealed her into the Hollow Knight , but ultimately it was not enough and the kingdom of Hallownest was razed by the plague.

The Nightmare's Heart

The Nightmare's Heart is the deepest power of the Nightmare Realm which exists at the heart of the Grimm Troupe . In the past, the nightmare realm became separated from the rest of the dream realm. The Ritual of the Grimm Troupe exists in order to provide the Heart with a vessel, which it needs to sustain itself. This vessel takes the physical form of Troupe Master Grimm or the Grimmchild .

Unn

Unn is a slug-like being who made the Mosskin and the vegetation of Greenpath from her dream. She was worshipped as a deity in Greenpath, but over time her strength faded. She sleeps in an area to the west of the Lake of Unn where she is calling to the Mosskin. The Mosskin also speak of a "Law of Unn" that is enforced onto wanderers who stray too far off the pilgrim’s way into Greenpath.

Void Entity

The Void Entity is stated by Godseeker to be the God of Gods and thus the most probable strongest being known. The Knight becomes the Lord of Shades after acquiring Void Heart and unifying the Void under their will. During the Embrace the Void ending, they become the focal point of the Void. It is unknown what kind of abilities they have other than being able to unify the Void and defeat the Radiance. During the cutscene after the Void Entity has killed Absolute Radiance in Godhome , it is shown to grab the Godseeker using Void Tendrils in the dream. It then bursts out of the Godseeker in the Junk Pit . Its motives are unknown even if it has underlying hints of malevolence portrayed in its actions.

Grand Mother Silk

Grand Mother Silk , also known as the pale monarch, is the higher being that rules over Pharloom and was the creator of the Weavers. The Weavers abandoned her an unknowable long period of time ago, with it being abundantly clear they wanted nothing to do with her, but Silk has spent the entire intervening time trying to forcibly return her first children to Pharloom. Her complete disregard for Pharloom has let the kingdom where she is worshipped as a god fall to ruin under a curse of undeath and cruel leadership of the Citadel , but she doesn't care about anything else than willing her envoys to retrieve her creation.

Other Powerful Beings

It is not certain whether these beings are higher beings, although they do appear to have powers beyond that of most ordinary bugs.

Gods of Thunder, Gods of Rain

Little is known about these beings that come from the homeland of the Godseeker, the Land of Storms . It is presumed that they were dead when the Godseeker came to be, as their silence was the catalyst to the Godseeker arriving in Hallownest to find other gods as well as the Land of Storms itself being referred to as a "godless land". Huge corpses can be found in the background of the Land of Storms - particularly two major ones found in the room with the Weathered Mask . These are known as "Old Gods" in the game's files and are possibly the Gods of Thunder and Rain. It can be noted that the Godseeker indirectly calls the Old Gods "pretender Gods", though it is unknown if she is actually referring to the Gods of Thunder and the Gods of Rain with this statement.

Vessels

The Vessels are the genderless children of the Pale King and White Lady , born of their attempts to seal the Radiance away. They have been hollowed out by the void in the Abyss , and are now found prominentely around Hallownest, though most reside as corpses in the birthplace. Notable vessels include the Knight, Broken Vessel, the vessel in Greenpath, and the Hollow Knight. Though both parents are confirmed higher beings, it is debated whether they themselves are higher beings, as they are not shown to have powers or abilities commonly attributed to higher beings. Regardless, they do seem to have abilities above those of common bugs.

Hornet

As the child of the Pale King and the Dreamer Herrah , Hornet has displayed physical prowess that eclipses most bugs due to her Wyrm and Weaver lineage. However, a part of her strength can also be attributed to her intensive training by Queen Vespa at the Hive . She has not demonstrated any Wyrm abilities thus far and appears to have only inherited her father's long lifespan. Even so, she is still considered to be a pale kin by characters like the Snail Shamans of Pharloom and is described to have the instinctive desire to rule over others like all higher beings. Though Hornet has admitted to her innate nature, she has also voiced a desire for cooperation over dominance and is capable of rejecting her instincts in most situations. Hornet often goes out of her way to help settlements and wayfarers, displaying a charitable and empathetic side that contrasts her stern outward persona. This has earned her the admiration and adoration of many, including her sibling the Knight .

Through her Weaver heritage, Hornet has also inherited a number of Silk-based abilities that are related to the pale monarch Grand Mother Silk . She can produce Silk within her body and use it to perform various techniques. One of her core ability is the power to bind Silk. She can bind her wounds with Silk to heal and even bind other Silk-related beings to herself to obtain their powers. Through binding, Hornet has gained the holy talent to play the Needolin from a Weaver husk. She can string Silk along her Hivesteel needle and use it as an instrument to play ancient melodies with enigmatic powers. One melody, titled Elegy of the Deep , allows Hornet to send her consciousness into another's memories like the Dream Nail . She can also manifest objects from the memories if she chooses to do so. Despite these powerful abilities, Hornet lacks the power her Wyrm father had to be considered a true higher being. However, it is possible for Hornet to complete her ascension into godhood by binding another higher being—such as the pale monarch—to herself. Doing so would trigger metamorphosis and alter Hornet's form permanently as seen in one of Silksong 's endings. Hornet has shown displeasure toward the idea and seeks to avoid it if possible.
