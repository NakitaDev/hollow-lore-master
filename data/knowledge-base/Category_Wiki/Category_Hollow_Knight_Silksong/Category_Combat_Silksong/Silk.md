# Silk

Source: https://hollowknight.fandom.com/wiki/Silk

Silk is a unique force that acts as the life-force for bugs in Pharloom . Similar to its counterpart, Soul , it can be manipulated into spells and healing, but it can also be used to power up machines and automatons.

In-game, Silk is represented on the HUD by a spool with several strands. Hornet starts with a broken spool, only able to hold 9 strands of Silk. When the maximum amount of strands are reached, they glow a white light. Silk accumulated in the extended Spool is smaller and more dim, but function the exact same. When Hornet dies, her Spool becomes broken and can only hold 9 Strands of Silk.

Lore

Silk is a beneficial force that can extend life and heal wounds that is found in almost every bug in Pharloom . Strands of Silk can be seen in places such as the Mossy Grotto , and when unlocking Silk hearts , they form around in the air before combining into the Heart. Bugs of Pharloom , especially those in the Whiteward , stored Silk in spindles, and have also recorded certain surgeries for injecting Silk into bugs.

Silk can be used to channel Silk Skills, such as Silkspear , Thread Storm, Sharpdart and Rune Rage which were made by the Weavers. Other skills such as Cross Stitch come from a special technique from Phantom , and Pale Nails which is a move created by the Grand Mother Silk herself. Many tools interact with Silk as well. Injector Bands increase the speed of binding and Spool Extender allows one to hold more Silk.

The bugs in Whiteward used Silk as a means to extend the life of their surrounding bugs, but generations of surgery lead to the Grand Mother to gain control over them, even while asleep. Many bugs and clans attempted to resist the Haunting, with notable examples being the Green Princes of Verdania and the Skarr Clan. However, they have weakened and most of their inhabitants either died or fell victim to the Haunting.

Hornet uses her Needle to gain Silk by striking enemies, as well as using spindles across the Citadel and Webs found in places such as Mount Fay .

Usage

Silk can be used to cast Silk Skills or to Bind , which heals Hornet 3 masks in a single instance. When there enough Silk to cast a Silk Skill, if equipped, the icon of the Silk Skill glows white. If there is enough Silk to Bind, then the Silk Spool's threads glow White accompanied by a soft sound effect.

Binding requires 9 Silk and only heals 3 masks. This can be increased to 4 when using the Multibinder tool. Casting Silk Skills cost 4 Silk each, but can be reduced to 3 using the Egg of Flealia tool.

Some Ancestral Arts also require Silk. Each Art requires 1 Silk each, with the exception of Needolin since it uses Silk over time. Pressing Battle Stance also expends Silk unless you have the Throwing Rings equipped.

The Silkshot tool is the only tool that requires Silk to be used, using 1 Silk per attack.

Acquiring Silk

Attacking Enemies

Enemies struck with the Needle cause Hornet to gain Silk at a rate of 1 strand per hit.

Silk Spindles

Silk Spindles are found across all of Pharloom . When hit with the Needle, they release Strands of Silk that are absorbed, granting 3 Strand of Silk each time they are struck. After a certain number of hits, the Silk Spindle runs out and no longer gives Silk. They are similar to Hollow Knight's Soul Totems .

Webs

Webs are found all across Pharloom. There are webs on sets of vines, potentially left by Weavers . Each web gives 3 silk strands.

Tools

Certain tools may help with the acquiring of Silk, like Druid's Eye and Weavelight . Some tools increase the Spool length like Spool Extender .

Sylphsong

Essence of a being merged within. Silk will be restored when resting at a bench.

Requires using 18 Memory Lockets on any crest besides the Hunter's crest and obtaining all previous rewards from Eva . Works exactly the same as Salubra's Blessing in Hollow Knight .

Hot Springs

Only 2 Hot Springs exist in Silksong: one in the Citadel Spa and the other in the Flea Caravan . Generates Silk at the same rate as Sylphsong.

Silk Spool

Silk Spools are additional storage for Silk, represented as an extension to the original Spool. Hornet can collect 9 Silk Spools throughout the game by collecting Spool Fragments. 2 Spool Fragments are required to make one Silk Spool. Each Spool holds 1 strand of Silk.

If Hornet gains silk if the original Spool is full, it is instantly transferred to the next empty spool, starting with the closest leftmost one.

Unlike Hollow Knight, Silk does not need to be transferred from Spools to the Main Spool, and there is no delay for transferring Silk between Spools. Also, Hornet does not gain Silk at a reduced rate for the extended Spools.

Trivia

Silkspear , Thread Storm and Sharpdart are all moves used by Hornet in her boss fight in Hollow Knight . However, Silkspear and Sharpdart are quicker with different animations from the boss fight. Thread Storm remains the exact same, with the exception of spamming the attack move increases the duration. Battle Stance has the exact same animation as the one used in the boss fight. Beast's Crest changes the animation of Battle Stance to a more upright stance with her needle facing down and opposite of where Hornet is facing. Hornet's voice also becomes more raspy. Using Throwing Rings with Battle Stance does not expend silk and is identical to Shakra's idle after walking away from her.
