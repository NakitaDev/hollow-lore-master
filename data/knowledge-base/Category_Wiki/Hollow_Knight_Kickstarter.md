# Hollow Knight Kickstarter

Source: https://hollowknight.fandom.com/wiki/Hollow_Knight_Kickstarter

The Hollow Knight Kickstarter campaign was a crowd-funding project that ran from 19 November 2014 until 19 December 2014. In this month, a total of $58,893 AUD was raised to fund the development of Hollow Knight . It is no longer possible to donate any money to this campaign.

Pledge rewards

Backers could donate a certain amount of money which granted them rewards depending on how much they donated.

Funded stretch goals

$35,000: Funding goal $39,000: The White Palace . Filled with traps & challenges + 2 new bosses: Dusk Knight, King's Golem (These bosses were merged into the Kingsmould ). $44,000: 4 additional quests. More stories + an expanded cast of bizarre bugs + 2 new bosses. $50,000: Wii U version of the game (This was later changed to the Nintendo Switch). $56,000: 2nd playable character. With their own unique quests & abilities ( Hornet in Hollow Knight: Silksong ) . $62,000: Colosseum of Fools (Funded by PayPal backers). Challenge area + 4 new bosses + solve the mystery of the colosseum.

Unfunded stretch goals

$68,000: 3rd playable character. With their own unique quests & abilities (This was going to be Zote ). $75,000: PS Vita version of the game. $85,000: The Abyss . A massive new expansion for only the hardiest of adventurers + 4 new bosses.

Trivia

According to Team Cherry , the unfunded Abyss expansion stretch goal would have "taken players down into the lake of void, to explore the twisted space within." When later asked on Creative Hustler's Show in October 2017, it was stated that Team Cherry would not be working on the content planned for the Abyss expansion, citing that the world for the game was built without considering this expansion. If the stretch goal to have Zote as a playable character was reached, Team Cherry had an idea to have a mode in the game where everything the Knight did was "Zote-ified". Their nail would be weak, dashes could fail, and after being hit they would go flying like Zote in the Colosseum of Fools . Ultimately this was not added later on, as Team Cherry did not think it was worth the amount of work required for content that only a tiny percentage of players would encounter.
