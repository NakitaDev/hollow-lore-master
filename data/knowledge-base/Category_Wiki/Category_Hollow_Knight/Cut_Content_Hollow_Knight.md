# Cut Content (Hollow Knight)

Source: https://hollowknight.fandom.com/wiki/Cut_Content_(Hollow_Knight)

This page contains a list of cut content for Hollow Knight . Before the release of the game, some content was announced or shown that ultimately did not make it into the final release. Some cut content was never announced but can instead be found in the game's files.

Cut Areas

Most revealed areas did make it into the game, with only minor rooms being scrapped. However, there were some moderately sized areas that were entirely cut from Hollow Knight .

Cut Charms

In the reveal of the charms system, an image showing various charms was shown in a Kickstarter update . Some of these charms made it into the game, although others did not, and some have so far been left unnamed. Several of the cut charms seem to appear on each of the Charm Achievements .

The ability to "Glorify" charms was scheduled to appear in the Godmaster content pack, when it was still named Gods and Glory. It was cut because Team Cherry did not have the time to add it, and they had difficulty balancing a whole new system for new upgrades.

Cut Dialogue & Developer Notes

The game files of Hollow Knight contain various unused dialogue, notes left by Team Cherry , and other text that cannot be found in the game itself. This text can be extracted from the files with tools such as Asset Studio .

The canonical status of most of this text is debatable as it is not used in the game. However, there is some dialogue that is used in the game which is similar to a few unused lines.

Cut Enemies

Several enemies and bosses did not make it into the final game. Some of them can be encountered as NPCs or corpses instead, while others were merged into a single enemy.

Cut NPCs

Only two NPCs ended up not being used. These NPCs are not named and only make one or two appearances in promotional material.

Cut Spells & Abilities

A few spells and abilities that were shown before release were never added to the final game. Some of them were modified and included in Hollow Knight under a different name.

Cut Miscellaneous Content

Content such as unused Items or rooms which were cut or changed in the final release of the game.
