# Unn

Source: https://hollowknight.fandom.com/wiki/Unn

Unn is an NPC in Hollow Knight who can be found in a pool of acid in the Lake of Unn . She gifts the Shape of Unn Charm .

Lore

Unn is a higher being who is responsible for all life in Greenpath . The temple-like structure at the edge of the Lake of Unn was a place where she was worshipped. She once existed with land and dream and devotion, though over time her strength faded. Dialogue of the Moss Prophet suggests that the Radiance's light, and the Infection distorts the life Unn created.

Unn may have at a time long ago been able to manipulate Void to create life, and may have this power still. Her power is attuned to creatures of Void, like the Knight. The Mosskin were made from the dream of Unn. Unn is calling to the Mosskin, who are waiting for the call to return.

In-game events

Unn is located in a pool in the southwest portion of the Lake of Unn. Landing next to this pool will cause her to rise up, stare at the Knight for a moment, and then spit out the Shape of Unn Charm before sinking back into the water.

Unn can be seen in Godhome in the Pantheon of Hallownest as a sleeping mass. Godseeker mentions that she can barely feel Unn's presence anymore.

Location

Unn can be found in a secret room under the Lake of Unn.

Trivia

It is widely accepted that Unn's gender is female because Moss Knight's Dream Nail dialogue tells us so. However, it is interesting to note that, while Moss Knight's line was not changed, most of the translations refer to Unn's gender as masculine/neuter. The evidence of that is in another line, where Mosskin speak directly to Unn: "Hidden Unn, we need you now." Russian , French , Spanish , Portuguese translations refer to Unn as masculine/neuter . Of all translations only German one speaks about Unn as a female creature . It is highly possible that, in fact, translators knew the true Unn's gender when they were working on that line but did not change Moss Knight's Dream Nail dialogue either because of a misunderstanding (probably not all of them understood the reference to Unn) or because of unwillingness to correct the English original text.
